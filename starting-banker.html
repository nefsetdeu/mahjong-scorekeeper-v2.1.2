The Starting Banker

The player who drew the East tile when assigning seats throws the two dice into the center of the walls.

Starting with East and going counter-clockwise, count around the table up to the combined dice number. The player who the count lands on will be the first EAST SEAT and STARTING BANKER. In the first hand of the game, they will be known as Double East (i.e. East round, East seat).

Example:

If the dice shows 4 and 3 (total: 7), starting from East, count 7 positions counter-clockwise:

  1. East (starting point)
  2. South (1)
  3. West (2)
  4. North (3)
  5. East (4)
  6. South (5)
  7. West (6)
  8. North (7) - becomes the Starting Banker

In this example, the North player would become the first East Seat and Starting Banker.
